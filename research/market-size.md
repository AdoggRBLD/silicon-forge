## TAM — $680M

The total universe of people who have demonstrated they will engage with hardware, logic, or engineering-adjacent content is roughly 45 million:

| Segment | Size | Source / Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Working FPGA / IC / embedded engineers | ~3.5M | US BLS estimates ~600K, scaled ~5× globally |
| Enrolled CS / EE students | ~10M | Global undergraduate + early-career enrollment |
| Active maker / hobbyist users | ~25M | Conservative subset of 90M+ Arduino + Raspberry Pi units shipped |
| KSP-style crossover players | ~5–10M | Non-engineers who gravitate to technical simulation |

At a modest $15 lifetime value, that's ~$675M.

This is Silicon Forge's universe if it executes perfectly across all markets and languages.

---

## SAM — $95M

Realistically, an English-first PC + mobile launch in years 1–3 reaches about 15% of the TAM universe. Language barriers, platform availability, and awareness filter the rest out.

- **Reachable population:** ~7M people
- **Blended ARPU (free + paying):** $13.50
- **SAM:** ~$95M

The maker community is unusually well-organized for targeted outreach. Hackaday, Adafruit, EEVblog, and the major electronics subreddits have proven advertising and community-marketing channels. The reachable cohort is concentrated, not diffuse — which is rare and good.

## SOM — $4.2M (3-Year Cumulative)

This is the "no viral breakout, well-executed indie launch" base case.

**Comparable:** Kerbal Space Program built 2–5M owners over years through word-of-mouth in a similar niche. Silicon Forge — newer, smaller team, narrower hook on first launch — can realistically acquire ~300K downloads over three years.

**Conversion assumptions:**
- 14% paying conversion (slightly below Steam's ~20% average; discounted for the technical complexity barrier)
- $100 ARPU (one-time purchase plus optional DLC, or equivalent subscription LTV)

**Math:** 300K × 14% × $100 = ~$4.2M cumulative

**Year 1 alone:** $600K–$900K is the realistic target.

## The KSP Analog Is the Most Important Data Point

There's no published demographic breakdown of KSP players, but forum surveys and educator usage data consistently suggest **roughly 65–70% of players are non-engineers** who became interested in aerospace *through* the game.

KSP still has 13–14K concurrent Steam players on an average day, and spiked to 20K+ when Artemis 2 launched last month — **eleven years after release.**

That long-tail retention is what Silicon Forge should be designed to replicate. Not the short-burst engagement of a typical mobile puzzle game.

---

## Mobile F2P — The Wild Card

The mobile puzzle/strategy market is $29.7B combined. That's context, not a direct target — unless Silicon Forge ships a mobile F2P version, in which case:

- **0.01% capture** of that market = ~$3M/year
- This single decision changes the SOM conversation entirely
- The risk: F2P design pressure can push the game away from teaching real engineering

Recommendation: ship F2P mobile but protect the educational core. Use the trade-game wrapper as the F2P-friendly surface; keep the engineering depth as the conversion driver into paid tiers and hardware kits.

## Sensitivity Analysis

| Scenario | 3-Year Revenue |
|---|---|
| Pessimistic — 100K downloads, 8% conversion, $80 ARPU | ~$640K |
| Base case — 300K downloads, 14%, $100 | ~$4.2M |
| Optimistic — 1M downloads, 18%, $120 | ~$21.6M |
| Viral breakout (HN front page + EEVblog feature compounds) | $30M+ |

The realistic range is wide because indie game outcomes are bimodal — most plateau at base case, a few break out hard. Plan operations for base case. Build the marketing flywheel for viral.

---

## Strategic Takeaways

1. **TAM is large enough to justify the bet.** $680M with strong tailwinds in both FPGA hardware and gamified education.
2. **SAM is the right planning horizon.** $95M reachable in 3 years — you don't need to win the world.
3. **SOM is the operational target.** Build the company on the assumption of $4.2M cumulative; treat anything above as upside.
4. **KSP-style longevity is the design north star.** Optimize for 5-year retention, not 3-month spike.
